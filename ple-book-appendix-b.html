Appendix B — GPS KPI Library
The complete institutional and governance measurement library for the Post-Labor Economics Governance Process Standard
Coverage: 16 GPS categories · 198 KPIs · 5 fields per KPI (name, measures, type, method, target guidance)
Scope: Institutional and governance performance KPIs applicable across all post-labor institutional forms. Does not include program-level outcome measures specific to particular service types (food security, housing stability, employment) — those belong in institution-specific program evaluation frameworks.
Note on type: Leading indicators are inputs and process quality measures that predict future performance — they allow early intervention. Lagging indicators are output and outcome measures that confirm whether the system has worked. Both are necessary. Institutions that track only lagging indicators discover problems after they have compounded; institutions that track only leading indicators may be improving inputs without improving outcomes.

Three ways to use this library

  • Designing a new measurement system: start with the category health composite (3 signals per category) then add leading KPIs that require the least measurement infrastructure to operationalize immediately
  • Auditing an existing measurement system: compare current tracked metrics against the full KPI set and identify gaps in leading indicator coverage — most institutions under-invest in leading indicators
  • Preparing a governance body report: the health composite signals per category give a 10-minute review framework; follow with the lagging indicators for any category showing health concerns

Selecting KPIs by institutional maturity

  • Level 1 institutions: 3–4 KPIs per category, prioritize measurement method "administrative data" (lowest collection burden) and the 3 health composite signals
  • Level 2 institutions: 6–8 KPIs per category, add survey-based leading indicators for primary GPS priorities
  • Level 3 institutions: 10–12 KPIs per category, full coverage with quarterly review cadence for leading indicators
  • All levels: begin with the categories identified as primary priorities for the institution's archetype (see Appendix A cross-reference and Chapter 24)

GPS 1.0Collective Vision & Governance Strategy8 KPIs
Category outcome
A post-labor institution with strong Category 1.0 performance has governance structures that represent its community, make decisions transparently, hold leadership accountable, and renew themselves without crises. End of Appendix B — GPS KPI Library
198 KPIs. 16 categories. Five fields each. Organized for practitioners who need to know what to track — and why it matters that they track it.
On selecting a starting measurement set: Institutions beginning GPS measurement should not attempt to implement all 198 KPIs simultaneously. Start with: (1) the three health composite signals for each GPS category identified as primary priority for your institutional archetype (Chapter 24); (2) the administrative data KPIs that can be extracted from existing records without new data collection; (3) the annual community survey KPIs that can be consolidated into a single survey instrument covering multiple categories. A starting measurement system of 25–35 KPIs across 5–8 priority categories is more valuable than a nominally comprehensive system of 100+ KPIs that cannot be consistently collected and reviewed.

On leading vs. lagging balance: Most institutions default to lagging indicators because they are easier to collect from existing records. The leading indicators in this library — process quality measures, compliance rates, infrastructure assessments — are the ones that allow early intervention before lagging problems compound. A measurement system with exclusively lagging indicators produces accountability without foresight. Aim for a 60/40 leading-to-lagging ratio in any category's active measurement set.
